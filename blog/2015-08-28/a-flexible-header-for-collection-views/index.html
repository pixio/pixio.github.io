Users love to customize their experience...

for better or for worse. Everyone remembers MySpace and its lovely user-configured cursor trails and auto-playing music. Those were the days. With the advent of web 2.0, people have developed expectations for polish and style but they still want to stare at their own content. User content is the reason social sites, apps, and platforms even exist. So, the next time you are working on an app where you want to emulate things like the Twitter banner, look no further than PXFullBleedCollectionView.

PXFullBleedCollectionView

There are two primary components to this simple but flexible pod: PXCollectionViewHeader and PXFullBleedHeaderCollectionView.

PXCollectionViewHeader is what you subclass to make your custom header view. You need to override minHeight and maxHeight in order to specify how large you want the header to be. After that, you can put whatever content you want into this header. Make sure that your constraints are flexible or your -layoutSubviews rects scale as the header resizes.

Next, use a PXFullBleedHeaderCollectionView in your view controller's view. This class has UICollectionViewDataSource and UICollectionViewDelegateFlowLayout properties that you use just like the normal ones on UICollectionView.

Boom! You now have a collection view that can do this:


Serving suggestion

Once you've experimented with this header a bit, try setting your navigation bar to clear and designing your header content to be partially underneath your nav bar buttons. With carefully selected or user provided images, this sort of UI can recover some screen space and let you show off more of your users' content.
