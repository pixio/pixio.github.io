The Remainder of my Grab Bag of Pods

There are four things left to show you from my bag of tricks. The first is a really simple view that lets you make a view with a gradient in it. The second lets you snapshot a preview of a website to an image. The third lets you screenshot a view with one line. The fourth and final one lets you draw a UIBezierPath of a convex polygon with rounded corners of a specified corner radius.

Let's go:

PXGradientView

This is a dead simple view. Basically, it takes away the pain of having to remember how to set up a CAGradientLayer. You can specify the direction the gradient should move (up, down, left, right), the colors and locations of those colors (no locations means even spacing for the colors), or you can use one of the two simple gradients (vertical transparent from clear to partially transparent black up or down).

This view lets you accomplish that oft-used look of putting text over an image and keeping it readable with a gradient to black underneath the text. You can also use it for any of your other axis-aligned gradient needs.

PXWebSnapshot

Have you ever wanted to render an image version of an arbitrary web page? No? Well, I did once. So I wrote a thing to make it easy to do over and over in case I ever wanted to do it again. I named it PXWebSnapshot.

Basically, it loads a webpage and takes a screenshot of it after it's done loading. You can specify the size of the screenshot with a CGSize and boom, a preview of a web page you can do all sorts of things with (like blur, or save, or upload).

UIView-Screenshot

What if you wanted to render a view to an image. Yes, I know it's pretty simple, but what if all you had to do was:

UIImage screenshot = [yourView screenshot];

With UIView-Screenshot you can. It's pretty simple. Once you have the image, you can blur it, tint it, do all sorts of stuff. Use your imagination!

UIBezierPath-RoundedPolygon

This is the most complex of the group. The interface, however, is very simple: specify an array of points and a corner radius. These points are then used to draw a rounded polygon composed of those points into a UIBezierPath. The corners of this polygon are rounded according to the corner radius you specify. The math only works for convex polygons, though, so if that doesn't fit your needs, you may want to look at the internals and see if you can adapt it. For what we needed, convex was enough.

Check out the example project to see how easy it is.
