Editionals

Radiate Media is a company trying to save print media from itself. Radiate Media has spent the last few years helping traditional small- and medium-size newspapers and magazines to have an online presence without all of the overhead.

Business Situation

Radiate Media had great success with their easy-to-use online content management system. It was a good fit for traditional editor’s workflows. Editors were now asking for a way to deliver the same digital experience to their reader’s smartphones and tablets. Radiate Media needed a way to be first to market with a mobile solution for their customers without an internal app development team.

Pixio’s Solution

Editionals was one of the very first publishing applications to take advantage of the iPad. The application needed to be memory efficient but contain all of the rich multimedia experiences enabled by the desktop web version of the Editionals content management system. This included HD video, HD audio, and rich, high resolution images that users could zoom in on.

Pixio developed a highly extensible multi-use application for Radiate Media. Editionals for iPad not only replicated the desktop web experience, but it improved upon it with beautifully fluid, animated user interface elements.

  • Client: Radiate Media
  • Service: Digital Publishing

LabWorks Mobile

Atlas Medical produces Atlas LabWorks, a tool for physicians and staff to handle medical laboratory work from end to end.

Business Situation

Quality care means access to clinical data 24/7. Improving clinical results means providing doctors with access to all of the information they need at the point of diagnosis. From the bedside of a patient to the golf course, Atlas Labworks provides doctors with instant access to vital information.

Challenges

Protected patient health information is extremely important to health care providers and their patients. While health care practices already follow HIPAA rules and regulations, practices must recognize that electronic health records (EHRs) present different challenges than paper charting systems.

Lab work has become increasingly valuable for diagnosing and treating patients and is used now more than ever before. One of the challenges in doing lab work is that patients want to know their results right away. Previously, doctors have had to wait for the results to come in via paperwork or wait until they were at their desk with a computer to review the information.

Solutions

Making sure that no protected patient information remains on the device while keeping the network-based application responsive is not an easy challenge. By architecting a transactional networking structure, Pixio helped Atlas Medical provide timely patient information in a smooth user experience.

Using Atlas’s secure server and API, Pixio developed a HIPPA-compliant mobile application that allows doctors to both see historical lab results and order new ones. The software allows doctors to view patient contact information so that they can communicate vital data to patients from any location with the tap of a button. If an additional test can help the doctor diagnose issues and help the patient, doctors can immediately order that test and track the results from a mobile device anywhere in the world.

Pixio and Atlas LabWorks are helping good doctors make good diagnoses as quickly as possible. This ultimately results in better healthcare for all of us.

  • Client: Atlas Medical
  • Service: Medicine

DME Logistics

Founded in 1997, Alpine Home Medical is a major durable home medical equipment company and service provider. Alpine Home Medical employs many of the best and brightest in the home medical equipment industry.

Business Situation

For any provider of physical goods, delivery trucks literally keep business moving. Having fully optimized logistics operations means greater throughput, more completed transactions per employee, and happier customers. As Alpine Home Medical began an aggressive expansion campaign and acquired competing firms, they sought a solution better than their existing paper-based delivery management system.

Challenges

After creating digital, database-backed versions of all of Alpine Home Medical’s existing logistics software, Alpine wanted to find an immediate way to drive more sales from the point of delivery, adding significant value to the bottom line through technology.

Early in the discussion, we discovered that Alpine wanted to use cellular-enabled iPads to stay in communication with their logistics server in real time. Delivery drivers needed to have remote access to orders, inventory, and forms. At the same time, the app couldn’t be dependent on a constantly active cellular connection due to inadequate coverage in some remote delivery locations. This presented the challenge of processing transactions without being able to communicate with the server.

Solutions

By automating logistics, capacity planning, and creating opportunities for up-sales, Pixio helped Alpine Home Medical with higher margins and increased profitability.

We solved the offline communications problem by introducing a proprietary syncing technology. Whenever the iPad is online, it syncs with the logistics server and retrieves updates to orders, inventory, and delivery routes. If the iPad goes offline, the driver still has access to orders and is able to complete deliveries and process transactions. When the iPad comes back online, any actions the driver made are replayed against the server to synchronize it with the state of the driver app. This feature allowed Alpine to keep track of their inventory in as-close-to-real-time as possible and maximized their awareness of daily operations.

  • Client: Alpine Home Medical
  • Service: Logistics

Pando

Purity LLC was founded by Pete Shirley, successful entrepreneur and former research scientist at NVIDIA.

Business Situation

After the (then) record-setting valuation of Snapchat, startups went crazy trying to chase the same market. At the same time, the Snowden revelations and iCloud data leaks brought encryption back into the public eye. Given Purity’s unique IP portfolio surrounding image processing, they felt that they had something to contribute to the wider discussion.

Challenges

Creating a clone of an existing application, even if you have something new to contribute, can be a path fraught with peril. Without something substantial to differentiate one’s product, it is an exercise in futility.

Purity had world-class expertise in image processing, but encryption and mobile are two areas where Purity did not have internal resources. Encryption is easy to get wrong and hard to get exactly right.

Solutions

Pixio and Purity collaborated to launch Pando as native applications for iOS (iPhone / iPad), Android, and Windows Phone. All three implementations use Purity’s unique image processing solutions. All three implementations also take advantage of state-of-the-art encryption. Pixio’s encryption implementations were certified by qualified experts — trusted university security researchers.

  • Client: Purity LLC
  • Service: Social Media / Messaging / Encryption / Security

dipityPix

Purity LLC was founded by Pete Shirley, successful entrepreneur and former research scientist at NVIDIA.

Purity was seeking to create an extensible photo application to take advantage of Purity’s unique intellectual property surrounding photos, photo filters, and automatic photo enhancement.

Purity was a very small team with a great idea to bring to consumers but they lacked the mobile experience to bring those ideas to one of the largest markets. When new and exciting camera features came to iOS, Purity recognized an opportunity; they needed quick development in order to gain a first-to-market advantage and leverage the new functionality.

Solutions

Pixio helped Purity engineer a seven app solution using a single, unified code base, allowing Purity to gain a large market footprint without the typical large costs of development. In addition, future changes and maintenance will integrate seamlessly across all seven applications simultaneously.

Pixio published multiple applications for the first iPhone, which had technical capabilities vastly inferior to today’s iPhone. This experience gave Purity the edge it needed to bring its ideas, normally meant for the desktop, to the much more limited iPhone without sacrificing the quality of Purity’s product.

  • Client: Purity LLC
  • Service: Consumer / Photography

Fill|Harmonic, Show|Ready, and Stage|Tracks

Right on Cue Systems (ROCS) provides officially licensed musical theater scores for local performances. They also provide a collection of digital tools a director can use to modify the show to fit his or her needs. A customer who has purchased a license for a show can digitally change the key and speed of musical pieces, cut out sections of songs, and perform other edits as appropriate for each show and each performer.

Business Situation

ROCS had desktop versions of Stage|Tracks and Show|Ready for several years. ROCS sought to grow their market share and create happier customers by bringing their desktop experience to mobile. A mobile version would enable directors to hold rehearsals and live performances in alternate venues. Going mobile meant they wouldn’t need their desktop on stage, but could still make edits and try them out on the spot.

Fill|Harmonic was a new system that ROCS was developing to allow their clients to “conduct” rehearsals, even if performers were not present. Conductors would be able to tap a Nintendo Wii Remote while conducting normally, and computer instruments would play in time with live performers.

Challenges

The greatest challenges for ROCS in bringing Stage|Tracks to mobile primarily revolved around digital rights management. In an era when any kid can jailbreak his or her parents’ smartphone, how could ROCS ensure that the licensed audio data would not wind up on the hottest torrent site by the end of the week? ROCS required a trusted partner.

Dynamic adjustments of digital scores also proved to be problematic. ROCS Show|Ready used a custom and proprietary MIDI-based format that allowed on the fly editing. ROCS needed a partner who had in-depth experience with musical formats, particularly since their MIDI-based format would not be natively supported on mobile devices, unlike MP3.

Finally, Fill|Harmonic needed someone to create smart algorithms that would allow the computer to not only follow a conductor, but to anticipate a conductor’s intent, behaving as if the computer was just like any other human performer.

Solutions

Pixio’s deep experience with encryption ensured that there would be no difficulty for ROCS in honoring their licensing agreements. Pixio also made sure that when a license expired, the music would disappear from the device without a trace.

Using a mobile phone as a theatrical performance audio player presented a more difficult challenge. Pixio provided solutions that allowed ROCS to maintain server-based control over playback of the audio content while avoiding the embarrassing and inevitable situation of someone calling or sending a text message to the device during a show. By leveraging an existing consumer platform like the iPhone or iPad, ROCS was able to help keep hardware and software costs low while expanding their market share significantly.

In order to tackle the custom MIDI format used for Show|Ready, Pixio developed a new MIDI playback and editing suite from the ground up using Apple’s Core Audio framework. The result was a full featured, dynamic, score editing suite with high quality sampled audio voices. This was no small feat.

ROCS needed a computer to follow the conductor as a human would. Pixio delivered a rock solid algorithm that could follow both the musical score and the conductor input to provide a lifelike feel that is otherwise impossible with digital score playback.

  • Client: Right on Cue Systems
  • Service: Music / Performance
