Users Usually Interact With Apps

Sounds simple. And it is. So I thought, "let's take something simple, and make it simpler." So I did.

But seriously, UI can be complicated and there are as many ideas about how users should indicate that do-the-thing should happen as there are things to do.

What I have this week are a few controls that I use over and over again and that fill a little bit more of that functionality gap between what iOS gives you and what your designer wants of you.

PXButton

What if I told you that you that you could center a side-by-side image and title in a button and it wouldn't make you grind your teeth in frustration when your text is so poorly aligned that you can't even remember what the last 6 characters were? I know right? Oh. So that's just me? Well, if you wanted that functionality anyway, here it is.

Meet PXButton. A button class with a lot to offer.

  • Obviously, it allows you to display an image, a title, or both, with the image on one side or the other of the title.
  • Beyond that, the font is a UI_APPEARANCE_SELECTOR so you can be consistent without being repetitive.
  • You can choose to have a nice color gradient behind your button. The class automatically creates a subtle gradient based on the background color you set. It can look good or look like iOS 3, depending on your color choice and taste.

There are a few other small things that are make the class a bit easier to use but aren't really worthy of a bullet point. Look at the header.

UIPlaceholderTextView

Let me start off by saying that this is not my code. I'm not here to take credit for the work of others online, just to throw more code at the very quickly growing mountain of libraries that is CocoaPods.

That being said, I use StackOverflow.com. It's great, as you most likely already know. Sometimes, though, it goes beyond mere greatness and someone will post a fantastic answer to a question. An answer that doesn't just address the points in the question but becomes a beacon of hope and understanding for generations of engineers. Here are some of my favorites. And once upon a time, I turned one of these into a CocoaPod.

Say hello to UIPlaceholderTextView. It's like a UITextView, but it has a placeholder text property. You know, just like the built-in UITextField class. Hey Apple, this is a useful feature and the entire community would love it if you added it.

PXInsetTextField

In much the same spirit of the previous one, I added functionality that should exist but doesn't. UITextView has a property which allows you to inset the content from the edge. This one is also based on a StackOverflow question.

PXInsetTextField should give you the power to do something you always wanted to do with UITextField but couldn't. It did for me, at least.

PXIconTextView

This control might be a bit more niche than the last three. It was developed for a specific use case (the text entry view for a chat screen). The purpose of this control is to display a text field with a small icon in the top left corner. As text is entered into the field, it grows in height to accommodate the new lines until it reaches a maximum height you specify. It's pretty simple but also pretty specific. If this sounds like your cup of chips, it's there for you. Woooo. On to the next.

PXMaskButton

What if you wanted a button. But not just any button, a button that has an icon and some text and then cuts the icon and text out of the button and makes it transparent. What if you didn't have to implement drawRect: to do it? It'd be great right? I wouldn't know. But you can, if you want. All you have to do is use PXMaskButton.

Some other things you can do with it:

  • Have a border. This is great because this class can be used as a checkbox thing too. So clear with a colored icon and text and a border when unselected, and gradient with cut out text and image when it's not.
  • A font and font size that can be customized at a class level thanks to UIAppearance.
  • An optional gradient. Gradients can look good, Ive, live with it.
  • Use it as a checkbox. I alluded to this above and it's a good feature. Think of this: you can make a grid of these babies and it would be the best collection view ever.
